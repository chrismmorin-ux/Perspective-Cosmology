## Plain Language

Tegmark's Mathematical Universe Hypothesis says the universe IS a mathematical
structure, and observers are "self-aware substructures" (SASs) within it. But
Tegmark never properly defines what an SAS is — his definition is circular: "a
substructure is self-aware if it thinks it is self-aware."

Our framework's "perspective" concept does what Tegmark's SAS tries to do, but
rigorously. A perspective is a formally defined partial view of a mathematical
structure, with precise axioms governing how it works. Every property Tegmark
wants SASs to have — perceiving time, making predictions, having stability —
either follows from our axioms or has a clear mathematical formulation.

**One-sentence version**: Our "perspective" is what Tegmark's "self-aware
substructure" should have been — a rigorous definition of an observer within
a mathematical structure.

## Question

Can we show that the Perspective Universe framework provides a concrete
instantiation of Tegmark's MUH, with a rigorous observer definition that
Tegmark lacks?

## Background

Tegmark (2007) argues: External Reality Hypothesis (ERH) implies the universe
is a mathematical structure. Observers are "self-aware substructures" (SASs)
within that structure. But:
- SAS definition is circular (self-referential)
- No formal criteria for when a structure contains SASs
- No mechanism for how SASs perceive time or space
- The MUH posits ALL mathematical structures exist (measure problem)

Our framework:
- Posits ONE specific mathematical structure (the Crystal)
- Defines perspectives rigorously (maps pi: P -> V)
- DERIVES time from adjacency (not assumed)
- DERIVES physics from algebraic structure (not postulated)

## The Key Insight

Tegmark argues: ERH -> baggage-free description -> mathematical structure.

We argue: perspective axioms -> transition algebra -> division algebras ->
specific mathematical structure (the Crystal).

The deep connection: both start from the requirement that the description
be "baggage-free" (no human concepts). Tegmark takes this to mean ALL
mathematical structures exist. We take it to mean the MINIMAL structure
supporting all possible perspectives exists.

Our approach is more constrained and more productive:
- Constrained: one structure instead of infinitely many
- Productive: derives physics (SM gauge group, spacetime dim, etc.)
