## 1. Ontological Ordering

### The Question
What is fundamental, and what emerges?

### Current Working Model

```
FUNDAMENTAL (primitive)
    │
    ▼
  Perspective
    │
    ▼
  Dimensional structure (B) — "scars" from symmetry breaking
    │
    ▼
  Points (P) — where dimensions overlap
    │
    ▼
  Content, connectivity, etc.
```

### Key Insight (2026-01-26)

**Points are not fundamental.**

Points emerge as intersections of dimensional structure. A "point" is where multiple basis directions have non-trivial mutual content.

```
p ↔ S ⊆ B   where S = {bᵢ : content projects onto bᵢ}
```

**Dimensions may not be fundamental either.**

Dimensions might emerge from perspective-breaking-symmetry. Each nucleation of perspective creates an asymmetry; that asymmetry defines a direction; that direction IS a dimension.

### Implication

The "perfect crystal" outside has no internal dimensions we would recognize — it's undifferentiated symmetry. Our dimensions are the scar pattern left by perspective.

## 2. Origin of Perspective

### The Core Question

How does perspective itself come into existence?

### Candidate Mechanisms

#### 2.1 External Cause ("God-like touch")
- Something outside the perfect crystal introduces the first perspective
- **Problem**: What is "outside" an infinite perfect structure?
- **Where it might appear later**: If we need individual perturbations from outside the structure onto the structure after it exists

#### 2.2 Entropic Inevitability
- Infinite extent + infinite duration → any improbable event has probability 1
- Somewhere, somewhen, a fluctuation occurs
- That fluctuation IS the first perspective
- **Problem**: What is "time" before perspective exists? Fluctuation requires a medium.

#### 2.3 Self-Reference Instability
- A structure rich enough to represent itself must be incomplete (Gödel)
- The representation-of-the-whole cannot be the whole
- This incompleteness IS the first perspective
- **Implication**: Perspective was always implicit in sufficiently rich structure
- **Status**: Philosophically appealing, needs formalization

#### 2.4 Symmetry Instability
- Perfect symmetry is unstable (balanced pencil)
- The crystal MUST break somewhere — spontaneous symmetry breaking
- No cause needed; it's the lowest-energy configuration
- **Physics parallel**: Higgs mechanism, vacuum phase transitions

#### 2.5 Nothing Cannot Exist
- A perfect undifferentiated crystal is indistinguishable from nothing
- "Nothing" is self-contradictory (there exists a thing that is nothing)
- Therefore differentiation must exist
- Perspective is the minimal differentiation
- **Implication**: Perspective is necessary for the crystal to BE anything

### Working Hypothesis

**Perspective may be necessary rather than caused.**

The crystal doesn't "become" perspective-containing through some event. Rather, perspective-containing is the only coherent form of the structure. The "perfect crystal" is an abstraction that can't actually exist undifferentiated.

### Open Questions

1. Can we formalize "self-reference necessitates perspective"?
2. Is there a mathematical structure where "too much symmetry" is provably unstable?
3. What distinguishes the first nucleation from subsequent ones?

```
1. The "Crystal" — undifferentiated potentiality
2. Perspective — the capacity for partial access
3. Nucleation — the process by which perspective breaks symmetry
```

### What Emerges
```
From nucleation:
  → Dimensions (directions of symmetry break)
  → Basis structure B

From dimensional overlap:
  → Points P (where dimensions intersect)
  → Locality (nearness = shared dimensions)

From perspective interaction:
  → Overlap γ (shared dimensional access)
  → Adjacency (which perspectives can interact)
```

### What We Don't Know
