## The Question

**O7**: "You're retrofitting known physics. What does the framework actually PREDICT?"

A genuine prediction must be:
1. **Novel**: Not already known from other physics
2. **Specific**: Precise enough to be testable
3. **Derivable**: Actually follows from the framework (not just asserted)
4. **Falsifiable**: Could be proven wrong

## Honest Assessment

### What the framework HAS:
1. Consistency with known physics (QM, GR, SM roughly)
2. Potential novel predictions in intermediate-γ regime
3. A conceptual framework for unification

### What the framework LACKS:
1. **Genuinely novel, specific, testable predictions**
2. Derivations precise enough to distinguish from other approaches
3. Quantitative predictions that differ from existing models

### The Core Problem

The framework mostly "explains" things we already know:
- α ≈ 1/137 → fitted with n_EW = 5
- n_gen = 3 → known since 1990s
- QM and GR → assumed to emerge (not shown)
- Decoherence → similar to existing models

**This is retrofitting, not prediction.**

## What Would Constitute a Genuine Prediction?

A genuine prediction would be:

1. **Specific numerical value** not known from other physics
   - Example: "Decoherence rate for a 10⁶ amu molecule at 1 μm separation is exactly X Hz"

2. **Qualitative phenomenon** not predicted by other frameworks
   - Example: "At exactly the Compton wavelength, decoherence rate has a cusp"

3. **Constraint** that could be violated
   - Example: "No stable particle can have mass between X and Y"
