## Key Findings

1. **Large-N intercept = 10/3** [CONJECTURE]: m(0++, N=inf) = Im_H + 1/Im_H = (Im_H^2+1)/Im_H = n_d - 2/N_c = 10/3 = 3.333. Matches lattice 3.37(15) at 0.2 sigma. The tree-level value n_d=4 is corrected by 2/N_c (planar leading-order).

2. **n_d - Im_O/n_c = 37/11 = 3.3636** [NOTED]: Even closer at 0.04 sigma, but lacks structural depth. The 10/3 form has 5+ equivalent framework expressions.

3. **Combined 0++ formula** [CONJECTURE]: m(0++, N) = 10/3 + dim_C/N^2 = 10/3 + 2/N^2. Chi^2 = 0.47 (4 points, 0 free params). Systematically better than n_d=4 for SU(3)-SU(5).

4. **Dressed 1+- at SU(3)** [CONJECTURE]: 10/3 + 2/9 + Im_H = 59/9 = 6.556. Between Chen (6.66, 1.6% error) and AT2020 (6.25, 4.9% error).

5. **Large-N 1+- tension** [KNOWN LIMITATION]: m(1+-) = 4+N diverges; lattice M_adj = 5.753(10) is finite. Regime of validity: N ~ 3-6 for exotic states, paralleling L>=2 breakdown.

6. **Band A classification** [DERIVATION]: All 6 SU(3) glueball gaps are 0.7-5.1% (5/6 strictly Band A, 2-+ at 0.7% borderline). Physical reason: pure gauge = only alpha_s corrections. No Band B/C expected.

7. **Base-only dressing asymmetry**: Dressed base improves 0++ (12% -> 0.9% avg across SU(N)) but worsens excited SU(3) states. Tree formula benefits from cancellation between overestimated base and implicit excitation corrections.

8. **Constituent model M(1+-) = 2*Im_H = 6**: At large-N in adjoint units, the 3-gluon constituent model gives M=6, exactly 2*Im_H. Matches lattice 5.753 at 4.3%.
